Astronomy and the Stars

Although I love what I do as an engineer, I find it increasingly important to have hobbies outside of the technical field. Astronomy is one of these hobbies and it was only recently I discovered how beautiful and fascinating it can be.

Observing the Sun

It all started with the sunset paper I was assigned in my Solar Systems class. As I took sunset pictures every other week and observed the Sun shift across the sky, I found myself looking at the sky more often. Every time I was at a red stoplight or outside after a long day, I took a look at the sky and just observed. I observed double rainbows, sun dogs, interesting cloud formations, and other objects I would have otherwise missed. What I also observed, was other people not noticing the same things I was.

The Camping Trip that Left Me in Awe

After some time, I heard about the Astronomy Club and decided to join since I enjoyed looking at the night sky. It was when I attended the dark sky camping trip to Deerlick Astronomy Village and used the Howey rooftop observatory that I truly appreciated the stars. It was the first time I saw so many stars at once and my first time feeling so awe-inspired that I had trouble describing how I felt. The sight made me feel humble and small. Without a doubt, watching the stars with other club members was truly a unique experience.

Art Portfolio

Art and Anime

Pencil drawing is a mode of art that allowed me to express myself in ways programming could not. I picked up drawing in middle school and decided to draw anime because I was a big fan of it. Ever since, I have been drawing on my free time for myself and sometimes my friends. Over the years, I have created 40+ sketches (mostly about anime) and scanned some of them onto my DeviantArt account for everyone to see.

Self-Improvement

Looking back, I am very proud of how far I've come since I first started. Every artist was first an amateur and that definitely applied to me as well. The character proportions looked awkward and I found it difficult to see the finished picture before I started a sketch. But despite all this, I remember enjoying the struggle of creating a finished piece. Each piece I finished showed growth and was something I could proudly say I drew. Although I am not a professional by any means, I enjoy drawing. I am completely immersed while working and completely satisfied when I finish.

My Art Today

Today, I draw when time allows or when I want to make a present for my close friends. The only thing that makes me happier than completing a piece is when others enjoy it as well. Even though I can't draw as often as I used to, I am still able to find the same focus and satisfaction as before.
